We receive this data from different sources depending on the feature. For example, some data comes directly from you, some comes from your device, some comes from sign-in providers you choose to use, and some is generated by service operation and related backend processing.

If you use a website form that prepares a deletion-request email, the information you enter is used locally to prepare that message. If you then send the email, we receive and process the contents you send through our support channel.

## 4. How location processing works

Location processing is a core part of Prxmity.

During ordinary active use, the app polls location periodically while the app is active. Based on the current service design, this is generally lower-frequency polling.

If you explicitly activate `radar`, the app uses higher-frequency precise polling to support the radar feature. On supported platforms, radar may continue while the app is backgrounded or the screen is off until you stop radar or fully close the app.

Tracking is intended to stop when you fully close the app. You can also stop higher-intensity tracking by deactivating radar.

We do not describe the service as constant always-on tracking after full app closure.

## 6. Legal bases

Depending on the processing activity, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases under applicable data protection law:

- performance of a contract, where processing is necessary to provide the service you requested
- legitimate interests, where processing is necessary to operate, secure, defend, or improve the service, protect users, handle requests safely, or prevent misuse
- consent, where you voluntarily ask to receive optional release-related communications or similar non-core updates

For core service functions, including account operation and core location-based discovery features, processing is generally based on providing the service you requested.

For security, abuse prevention, legal acceptance evidence, age-gate enforcement, logging, request verification, and related operational controls, processing is generally based on legitimate interests.

For optional release-interest signups and related release communications, processing is generally based on your request to receive those communications and, where applicable, your consent.

Mobile operating-system permissions, such as location or notification permission, are operational controls. Granting or denying a device permission may affect whether a feature works, but device permission is not the same thing as the legal basis under data protection law.

If you do not provide required authentication data, or if you do not grant required location permissions, core features or parts of the service may not function properly or may not be available to you.

## 9. Retention

We keep different categories of data for different periods depending on product need, safety, security, legal, and operational requirements.

Based on the current service configuration, examples of configured retention windows include:

- location data retention controls around 14 days
- geo session retention controls around 30 days
- encounter-history retention controls around 30 days
- some incomplete or unready account states retained for shorter periods
- dormant account retention controls around 360 days

These operational windows may change over time if the service changes.

Release-interest records are retained only as long as reasonably necessary for release coordination, test-group invitations, research outreach, important product updates, opt-out handling, or related operational follow-up.

Support, privacy, and deletion-request communications are retained only as long as reasonably necessary to handle the request, verify identity where needed, prevent abuse or unauthorized deletion, document the outcome where legally justified, or resolve security, legal, or dispute issues.

Some records, including legal acceptance evidence, security records, or logs, may be retained for different periods where reasonably necessary.

## 10. Account deletion and request channels

If you still have access to the app, the preferred deletion method is the in-app account-deletion controls we make available.

If you cannot access the app, you can contact `support@prxmity.com` or use public website request channels that help you prepare and send a deletion-request email.

If you delete your account, Prxmity is intended to remove your backend account data and corresponding authentication identity records associated with the service.

Website deletion-request flows do not authenticate you by themselves, and entering an email address alone is not treated as proof of account ownership. We may need to verify identity before processing a deletion request.

Deletion may not mean that every residual technical trace disappears instantly from every system, cache, log, backup, or provider layer, but the intended production behavior is account removal rather than mere deactivation.

We may retain limited data where reasonably necessary for security, legal compliance, dispute handling, or technical integrity.
