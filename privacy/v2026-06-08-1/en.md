# Proximity Privacy Policy

Status: Draft content for integration, staging, and product review.

This draft privacy policy exists so product builds can display a realistic, scrollable, versioned privacy notice before final legal approval. It reflects the categories of data the service is expected to handle and the operational reasons those categories may be used, but it remains subject to legal and compliance review.

By using a build that references this repository, you acknowledge that the document is provisional and may change before a production launch or wider public rollout.

## 1. Data categories we expect to process

Depending on the feature set enabled in a given build, the service may process account identifiers, authentication provider information, profile metadata, language preferences, coarse or device-provided location signals, app interaction events, support communications, and device-level diagnostics needed to keep the product functioning.

Some features may also rely on temporary state that indicates whether you have completed onboarding, accepted the latest legal documents, granted required permissions, or interacted with other users through the product.

## 2. Why we expect to process data

We expect to process data to authenticate users, personalize language and onboarding flows, determine proximity-related product behavior, support safety checks, investigate abuse, maintain service reliability, and improve product quality during development and release preparation.

In limited cases, operational logs or moderation-related records may also be kept to understand incidents, reproduce errors, respond to reports, and document enforcement decisions.

## 3. Location-related signals

Because Proximity is built around nearby discovery features, some builds may use device-provided location or related derived signals to determine whether relevant product interactions should be shown. The exact precision, retention period, and server-side handling remain subject to product and legal review.

This draft is intentionally cautious: it describes expected categories and purposes without claiming a finalized production implementation where none has yet been approved.

## 4. Retention approach

We expect to keep different categories of data for different periods depending on product need, safety requirements, operational debugging, legal obligations, and account lifecycle state. Some information may be deleted or anonymized when it is no longer required, while other records may need to be retained longer to document incidents or security events.

Specific retention schedules will be finalized in a later approved version.

## 5. Sharing and service providers

We may rely on infrastructure, authentication, analytics, customer support, hosting, or security providers to operate the service. Where providers are used, we expect access to be limited to what is reasonably necessary for the relevant function and subject to contractual and security controls appropriate to the final implementation.

We do not intend this draft to imply any finalized provider list or completed production data-flow map.

## 6. User controls

Depending on the product surface, users may be able to update profile information, switch interface language, review legal document versions, revoke certain permissions, or request account deletion. The exact controls available in production may differ from what is available in a development or staging build.

## 7. Draft status

This document is a working draft intended to support integration and review. It is not a final production privacy notice and should not be treated as the last approved statement of how the live service processes personal data.
